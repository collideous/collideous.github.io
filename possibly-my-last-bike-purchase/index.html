Possibly My Last Bike Purchase

Patrick June 7, 2025

I recently bought a second Marin Gestalt X10 to build my X12G. The X12G is very similar to my 2023 X12B. I set it up with several on-hand parts, both used and new, and a bunch of newly purchased ones, just like I always do. I turn 56 this year, and the Gestalt X12G is possibly the last bike I built. I have a fleet of seven complete bikes and an incomplete road bike, the Ritte Snob Disc. There's a fat-bike, a single-speed, and a 27.5+ geared MTB for trail riding. Then, there are four drop-bar bikes for road and gravel. All of them can be ridden for several more years. If I rebuild the Ritte Snob Disc and ride each of my drop-bar bikes for 3 years, that's 15 years. I'll be 71 years old.

Since winters are getting shorter and snow is getting rarer, my 44 Snakedriver fat-bike should be with me for years. My 44 Marauder single-speed has 3,700 kilometers. Thanks to sliding dropouts, it could be turned into a geared 29er if I ever wanted to. The Stooge MK5 can also be ridden for many more years. At present, it only has 2,250 kilometers on the odometer. I can easily put another 20,000 kilometers on all three combined. That will take a while.

The X12B and X12G will share road and gravel duties. The older X12B will be ridden from fall to spring, while the newer X12G will be used from spring to fall. The Nordest Albarda and Volagi Viaje, which I completely rebuilt, will return to action after the two Marins reach the end of their service lives. Both have more traditional geometries that allow bike fit changes that may be necessary as I age. A lot can be done on those two bikes with reduced saddle setbacks or shorter and taller stems.

I rebuilt my Ritte Snob Disc in April 2024, only to take it apart a year later. It's without brake/shift levers, derailleurs, and cranks. I may reassemble it at the end of the year or next, though I'm not yet sure how. If I go 2x12, I have cassettes and chainrings lying around. If I opt for 1x12, I would need a few more things. Either way, I will likely opt for SRAM Force AXS, the yet unreleased 2x12 groupset, or the current 1x12 XPLR edition. Orange Raketa cranks would be my preference for a 1x12 setup, and Rotor Aldhu carbon cranks in the case of 2x12.

So, while my Marin Gestalt X12G may be the last bike I built, I won't be done wrenching any time soon. It's nice to think that the fleet I have is the fleet I'll be riding into old age. Whenever cycling news sites reprint manufacturer's press releases, I can skip them all. With a basement full of bicycles, the future is well provided for. N+1 is all but history. Stay tuned for an introduction to the X12G, maybe a post about the differences between the 2023 and 2025 Gestalt X, and a future post about my return to tubes, albeit modern-day TPU tubes.
