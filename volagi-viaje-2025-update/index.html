Volagi Viaje 2025 Update

Patrick February 5, 2025

The creme-colored Volagi Viaje SL steel bike saw several updates in 2023 and 2024. The first round of changes was in October 2023, when I switched it from a 2x12 to a 1x12 drivetrain. In March 2024, I swapped derailleurs, and in October, I replaced the Hope calipers. I have a new project in mind for 2025 for which I needed some of the Volagi's components. In doing so, I decided to disassemble my Ritte Snob Disc. I moved the carbon Rotor Aldhu cranks, the spider, the SRAM Red derailleur, the saddle, and the seat post from the Ritte to the Volagi. I also bought a new 4-bolt, 44T Rotor QRing, and a WolfTooth LoneWolf chain guide.

The 4-bolt, 44-tooth, oval Rotor chainring is a work of art. Its CNC-machining is pure perfection. Designed as a dedicated 1x ring, the chain line is moved inward by millimeters. There's ample chainstay clearance, so I love that, as it minimizes the cross-chain effect when riding in the larger cogs, which I use far more than the smallest two cogs.

I replaced the newer Zipp seat post with the older generation post purely for aesthetics. The fork, Thomson handlebar, and Rotor cranks all have a high-gloss finish, and the older, glossy post better matched this. All I have to do now is ride it.

At first, I thought about selling the Ritte Snob Disc frameset and the Industry Nine wheelset, but I'm not ready to part with this bike yet. Instead, I decided to pack it into my travel case. Recently, I saw spy shots of the new SRAM Force road groupset published online. Like the new Red groupset, Force remains a 2x12 configuration. The new Force components would look great on the Ritte, so why not reassemble it in a year or two with those parts?

Volagi Viaje 2024 Update

I love seeing bike technology advance. I don’t adopt everything. In fact, a lot that’s supposedly “forced” on us isn’t my cup of tea. I’ll never buy a bike with fully internally-routed cables, for instance. I just won’t. But I love options. And options we have. Besides, when you can take an 11-year-old Volagi Viaje and throw the very newest components on it, where’s the reason to moan and groan? Standards haven’t changed all that much. You just need to be smart about the bikes and components you’re choosing.
