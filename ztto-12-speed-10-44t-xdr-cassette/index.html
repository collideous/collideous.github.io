ZTTO 12-Speed 10-44T XDR Cassette

Patrick January 25, 2025

Cassette options have been limited if you’re running a 1x12 XPLR drivetrain on a road or gravel bike. In XDR, SRAM offers the XG-1251 all-steel cassette as well as the XG-1271 with the largest cog in aluminum. There’s also the PG-1231 11-44T for Shimano driver bodies. They never made a Red-level 12-speed cassette to match their Red XPLR derailleur. There’s Garbaruk’s meticulously machined cassette. Eleven of the cogs are CNC-machined out of a solid block of steel. A 44T aluminum sprocket is bolted to that. At 280 grams, it’s the most lightweight gravel cassette. Priced at 280 euros, it costs a euro per gram. It’s a beautiful cassette, and I will certainly get one.

ZTTO now offers a lightweight, affordable alternative. The cassette’s construction is very similar to that from Garbaruk. 11 cogs (10-38T) are machined out of a solid block of high-strength steel. The 44T cog is made from 7075 aluminum and riveted to the two largest steel cogs. Here’s how the different cassettes compare:

ZTTO and SRAM use identical cog sizes. Garbaruk decided to have 1-tooth jumps for the first three gears, and 2-tooth jumps for the next three. Gear 6 through 12 are the same for both cassettes, except for the 11th cog. SRAM has a 38-teeth cog, while Garbaruk went one smaller with 37 teeth. I dislike the jump from 11 to 13 teeth on the SRAM cassette. The chain sitting on the 11T cog often feels too hard of a gear. Shifting up one gear then feels too much of a change. I’m missing that 12T in between. The gear changes on the Garbaruk cassette are possibly smoother. I use the 44T only on steep climbs when I’m too tired to ride out of the saddle. I mostly climb in the 28T and 32T and only use the 38T when it gets steep. Riding in the same fashion should be doable with a 37T.

My ZTTO cassette arrived well-packaged in a small cardboard box. No original packaging or instructions were included, but I don’t mind that, as packaging just creates unnecessary trash. The cassette has a claimed weight of 342 grams, but mine actually weighs only 325 grams. That's only 45 grams more than the Garbaruk cassette at a third of the price. The machining quality and overall finish of the cassette are outstanding. The saying used to be: "Weight, price, strength. Pick two". This no longer seems to apply to products like the ZTTO cassette. You get all three: light, good, and inexpensive.
