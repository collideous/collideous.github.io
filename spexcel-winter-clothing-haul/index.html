Spexcel Winter Clothing Haul

Patrick December 20, 2024

Finding myself more on the road than in the snow, my cycling wardrobe needed more suitable clothes for winter rides. When fat-biking, I typically wear a cycling jacket with a windproof vest, one or two long-sleeved shirts, warm tights, and waterproof cycling shorts. I prefer bib tights when riding on the road. My dresser had only two of those, one for rain, and one for cold weather. I needed more options, particularly for days with temperatures between 0° and 10°C. Happy with the quality of the Spexcel clothes I previously purchased, I turned to them to update my winter clothes. Over the past few months, I placed a couple of orders for:

The blue bibs with yellow straps were the first ones I ordered. The construction and the chamois of those bibs are outstanding. The only thing that could be better is the leg hem. It could be tighter. It isn't an issue if you wear tall winter cycling shoes or overshoes. The dark brown ones I bought later have an elastic leg hem band with a printed silicone gripper. Bib tights used to have foot loops to prevent them from sliding up, but that seems to have fallen out of favor in recent years.

There are already plenty of vests in my dresser, but since that's a garment I wear year-round, one can never have enough. It's nice to have a bunch in various colors to match them to whatever else I'm wearing. The same can be said about long-sleeved jerseys. The shelf in my closet has a pile of them, some old, some recent. There are many from DHB, four MyKilometre purchased last year, and now two by Spexcel.
