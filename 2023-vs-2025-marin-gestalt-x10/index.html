2023 Vs. 2025 Marin Gestalt X10

Patrick July 22, 2025

Besides the obvious color change, the 2025 Marin Gestalt X10 has undergone slight changes from the 2023 model. The microSHIFT Advent X groupset was replaced by Sword, and the blackwall Vee Tire Rocket Man tires were swapped with skinwall 700Cx45 Quickstyx tires. Since I only used frame, fork, headset, and thru-axles, let's take a look at what changed between 2023 and 2025. The biggest change is the fork. It's an entirely new fork. It is beefier, and the fork legs have a more triangular shape. The thread in the fork crown to attach a fender moved from the front to the underside of the crown. The steerer tube of the 2023 fork had a 45° shoulder and doesn't require a race, while the 2025 fork uses one. Personally, I prefer the more rounded shape of the old fork.

The 2023 frame has massive tire clearance. As a result, chainring clearance is tight. Marin gave the 2025 frame slightly more chainring clearance. As a result, tire clearance had to be reduced a bit. The 55 mm wide René Herse Antelope Hill tires still fit, though. It would have been neat to future-proof the frame with a UDH dropout, but I can live with that. I will be running 1x12 drivetrains for the next couple of years.

Well, that's everything already. It was an excellent bike when it was released, so not much needed to be changed. The increased chainring clearance offers people more options with chainring sizes or chainline. I was happy with the chainstays of the 2023 model. At the end of the year, I'll replace the SRAM Red brakes with the new Force brakes. Not because the bike needs them, but because I'd like to move the brake levers back to my Ritte Snob Disc, from which I stole them for the X12G.
