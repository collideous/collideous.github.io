Three Months Of Riding With The DJI Neo

Patrick December 25, 2024

If you haven’t read my previous posts about the DJI Neo, check out my introduction and why I think it is the ideal drone for cyclists. I’ve owned the Neo for three months and brought it along on several road rides and, more recently, fat-bike rides in the snow. I use an EVOC Hydro Pro 3L bike vest and a small SunnyLife protective case to carry the DJI Neo while riding. The case only fits the Neo, measures 175x156x76 mm, and weighs 205 grams. Sunnylife now also makes a butterfly body bag for the Neo, weighing only 73 grams. The best places to get them are AliExpress or Temu.

While I strongly discourage using the Neo on roads where you might encounter other traffic, I have found that it’ll have no issues with a car if it follows you far and high. Just move to the side of the road; the Neo will do the same. In early November, DJI released firmware update V01.00.0400 that increased the speed in tracking mode from 20 km/h to over 32 km/h. On flat farm roads, where I’ve used it, the Neo can more easily keep up with me if there’s no headwind.

Lots of snow blanketed Switzerland the week of November 20th. This was the first opportunity to test the Neo in the snow. I brought it with me on multiple occasions, and it performed flawlessly. On a recent fat-bike ride in Les Prés d’Orvin, a young couple asked me about it when they noticed the Neo behind me. The young woman had seen a travel influencer on TikTok using one. After watching the Neo land in my palm and hearing how easy it is to operate and how inexpensive it is, the young woman told her boyfriend she would get one. It truly is a cool little tool.
