Possibly My Last Bike Purchase

I recently bought a second Marin Gestalt X10 to build my X12G. The X12G is very similar to my 2023 X12B. I set it up with several on-hand parts, both used and new, and a bunch of newly purchased ones, just like I always do. I turn 56 this year, and the Gestalt X12G is possibly the last bike I built. I have a fleet of seven complete bikes and an incomplete road bike, the Ritte Snob Disc. There's a fat-bike, a single-speed, and a 27.5+ geared MTB for trail riding. Then, there are four drop-bar bikes for road and gravel. All of them can be ridden for several more years. If I rebuild the Ritte Snob Disc and ride each of my drop-bar bikes for 3 years, that's 15 years. I'll be 71 years old.

Videos Shot With The DJI Neo

You can't let it rip when riding downhill with a DJI Neo in tow, particularly in the woods. I slow my speed and adjust my lines with the drone in mind. If branches block the Neo's flight path, I slow way down. If I ride around a tree, I slow down and ride around it the farthest possible. It's not a tool to capture the action, but a great tool to capture the scenery.

Three Months Of Riding With The DJI Neo

If you haven’t read my previous posts about the DJI Neo, check out my introduction and why I think it is the ideal drone for cyclists. I’ve owned the Neo for three months and brought it along on several road rides and, more recently, fat-bike rides in the snow. I use an EVOC Hydro Pro 3L bike vest and a small SunnyLife protective case to carry the DJI Neo while riding. The case only fits the Neo, measures 175x156x76 mm, and weighs 205 grams. Sunnylife now also makes a butterfly body bag for the Neo, weighing only 73 grams. The best places to get them are AliExpress or Temu.

Patrick November 16, 2024

Mavic Cycling Shoes

I usually stay loyal to a brand when something works or fits well. When you look at my bike fleet, you’ll easily spot a bunch of those brands. There’s Rotor for cranks, SRAM for shifting, Hope for braking, and Prologo for sitting. You’ll see René Herse, WTB, and Zipp on multiple bikes. I have the same brand loyalty for everything I wear on the bike. I move on to other brands from time to time, but it always happens gradually. I used to wear Giro helmets exclusively in the past. While I still have two old ones, I’ve moved to Lazer for road and gravel and Smith for snow and dirt. On the opposite end of my body, my feet slip into the shoes of two brands. There’s Mavic for warm and dry days and Northwave for cold or wet days.
