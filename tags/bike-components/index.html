Volagi Viaje 2025 Update

The creme-colored Volagi Viaje SL steel bike saw several updates in 2023 and 2024. The first round of changes was in October 2023, when I switched it from a 2x12 to a 1x12 drivetrain. In March 2024, I swapped derailleurs, and in October, I replaced the Hope calipers. I have a new project in mind for 2025 for which I needed some of the Volagi's components. In doing so, I decided to disassemble my Ritte Snob Disc. I moved the carbon Rotor Aldhu cranks, the spider, the SRAM Red derailleur, the saddle, and the seat post from the Ritte to the Volagi. I also bought a new 4-bolt, 44T Rotor QRing, and a WolfTooth LoneWolf chain guide.

ZTTO 12-Speed 10-44T XDR Cassette

Cassette options have been limited if you’re running a 1x12 XPLR drivetrain on a road or gravel bike. In XDR, SRAM offers the XG-1251 all-steel cassette as well as the XG-1271 with the largest cog in aluminum. There’s also the PG-1231 11-44T for Shimano driver bodies. They never made a Red-level 12-speed cassette to match their Red XPLR derailleur. There’s Garbaruk’s meticulously machined cassette. Eleven of the cogs are CNC-machined out of a solid block of steel. A 44T aluminum sprocket is bolted to that. At 280 grams, it’s the most lightweight gravel cassette. Priced at 280 euros, it costs a euro per gram. It’s a beautiful cassette, and I will certainly get one.

The P-Brakes That Came Years Before V-Brakes

The first bike components I designed and manufactured were a set of linear-pull cantilever MTB brakes. I designed them in the last year of my apprenticeship and CNC-machined them during summer break in my first year of college. The apprentice department where I did my apprenticeship let me use their machines. I made four tensioner nuts, washers, spring covers, brass sleeves, and steel bushings on a conventional Schäublin 102 tabletop lathe. The lever arms and a fixture to make them were machined on a CNC vertical mill - a Mikron WF31C with a Heidenhain TNC155 control. I worked on those and the larger WF51C during my apprenticeship. I loved working on those and was good at programming them.

Patrick October 13, 2024

Updating Brakes Round Two

Back in mid-July, I updated the Hope brakes on my Volagi Viaje. I replaced the original RX4 calipers with the new and improved RX4+. Whereas the old RX4 used 2x14 mm and 2x11 mm pistons, Hope increased the pistons to 2x16 mm and 2x14 mm for the RX4+ calipers. I paid little attention to the pads then, but once out of the box, I immediately realized that RX4 and RX4+ calipers shared different brake pads. The original pads for SRAM RX4 calipers are 30.8mm wide. The width of the pads increased to 37.0mm for RX4+ calipers. That's a 20% size increase.
