Living Up To The Site's Name

The past two weekends I lived up to the name of this blog. I fat-biked off-trail. On Sunday, January 12th I took my fat-bike to Les Prés d'Orvin for a loop to the Petit Chasseral. The snow conditions were spectacular. The weather however wasn't. It was foggy and a cold wind was blowing. I don't mind that. When I'm out fat-biking on snow, the snow quality is all that matters. The days ahead of the 12th were warm with rainfall. On Friday, the mercury started dropping and went well below zero on the weekend. That gave the snow a nice, hard crust thick enough to carry a cyclist and his fat-bike. This is the absolute best snow a fat-biker can wish for. There’s no need to follow trails of any kind. That's what I love, and that's what I did. The only thing that strong winds and fog don't offer is opportunities to shoot good photos.

Three Months Of Riding With The DJI Neo

If you haven’t read my previous posts about the DJI Neo, check out my introduction and why I think it is the ideal drone for cyclists. I’ve owned the Neo for three months and brought it along on several road rides and, more recently, fat-bike rides in the snow. I use an EVOC Hydro Pro 3L bike vest and a small SunnyLife protective case to carry the DJI Neo while riding. The case only fits the Neo, measures 175x156x76 mm, and weighs 205 grams. Sunnylife now also makes a butterfly body bag for the Neo, weighing only 73 grams. The best places to get them are AliExpress or Temu.

My Latest Gadget, The DJI Neo

Seven years ago, I bought a Yuneec Breeze 4K drone for cycling photography. It allowed me to snap some awesome shots only a flying camera can take. It was an unreliable flying object, though. After half a year, it self-destructed. I've been following the development of consumer drones as attentively as the bike market ever since. DJI brought the Spark and the Mavic Minis. US-based Skydio was the first company to release a drone with subject follow mode and obstacle avoidance with the R1 in 2018. They were never available to consumers in Europe. Last year, Zero Zero Robotics brought the HoverAir X1 pocket-sized self-flying drone. They improved this concept this year with the HoverAir X1 Pro/ProMax. While the 8K video-capable ProMax is a drone I could see myself owning one day, I'm getting back into drone photography on a much smaller budget with the DJI Neo.
