Possibly My Last Bike Purchase

I recently bought a second Marin Gestalt X10 to build my X12G. The X12G is very similar to my 2023 X12B. I set it up with several on-hand parts, both used and new, and a bunch of newly purchased ones, just like I always do. I turn 56 this year, and the Gestalt X12G is possibly the last bike I built. I have a fleet of seven complete bikes and an incomplete road bike, the Ritte Snob Disc. There's a fat-bike, a single-speed, and a 27.5+ geared MTB for trail riding. Then, there are four drop-bar bikes for road and gravel. All of them can be ridden for several more years. If I rebuild the Ritte Snob Disc and ride each of my drop-bar bikes for 3 years, that's 15 years. I'll be 71 years old.

My Favorite Road Tires

In the old days, road cyclists firmly believed that tires needed to be narrow and inflated to ridiculous pressures to be fast. 25 mm wide tires used to be the de facto standard. In more recent times, many warmed up to 28 mm tires. While René Herse has been testing and comparing much wider tires for many years, bike publications have only very recently discovered that wide tires don't slow you down. Cyclingnews now says that "40mm road tires are faster for nearly everyone".

Videos Shot With The DJI Neo

You can't let it rip when riding downhill with a DJI Neo in tow, particularly in the woods. I slow my speed and adjust my lines with the drone in mind. If branches block the Neo's flight path, I slow way down. If I ride around a tree, I slow down and ride around it the farthest possible. Volagi Viaje 2025 Update

The creme-colored Volagi Viaje SL steel bike saw several updates in 2023 and 2024. The first round of changes was in October 2023, when I switched it from a 2x12 to a 1x12 drivetrain. In March 2024, I swapped derailleurs, and in October, I replaced the Hope calipers. I have a new project in mind for 2025 for which I needed some of the Volagi's components. In doing so, I decided to disassemble my Ritte Snob Disc. I moved the carbon Rotor Aldhu cranks, the spider, the SRAM Red derailleur, the saddle, and the seat post from the Ritte to the Volagi. I also bought a new 4-bolt, 44T Rotor QRing, and a WolfTooth LoneWolf chain guide.

Patrick January 25, 2025

ZTTO 12-Speed 10-44T XDR Cassette

Cassette options have been limited if you’re running a 1x12 XPLR drivetrain on a road or gravel bike. In XDR, SRAM offers the XG-1251 all-steel cassette as well as the XG-1271 with the largest cog in aluminum. There’s also the PG-1231 11-44T for Shimano driver bodies. They never made a Red-level 12-speed cassette to match their Red XPLR derailleur. There’s Garbaruk’s meticulously machined cassette. Eleven of the cogs are CNC-machined out of a solid block of steel. A 44T aluminum sprocket is bolted to that. At 280 grams, it’s the most lightweight gravel cassette. Priced at 280 euros, it costs a euro per gram. It’s a beautiful cassette, and I will certainly get one.
