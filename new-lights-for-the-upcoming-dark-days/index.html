New Lights For The Upcoming Dark Days

Patrick October 10, 2025

My seven-to-five work schedule makes me an evening cyclist. During the daylight savings period, the sunset is usually late enough to end the day with a good ride. Once we head into October and turn the clocks back at its end, I can only leave the house with bike lights.

I've been using Magicshine lights since 2009. I bought my first one from GeoManGear, which played a significant role in bringing affordable lights to everyday riders like myself. I used two MJ-808E 1000-lumen lights for several years. On my drop-bar bikes, I typically installed both and velcroed the batteries to the underside of my handlebars. On my 44 Big Boy, the two lights were attached to the bars, and the two batteries were securely attached to the fork legs with velcro straps. This setup changed with the arrival of my 44 Snakedriver fat-bike. I had a special carbon plate made to go atop my Jones H-Bar Loop handlebars. It was a simple way to pop the lights on and off the bike. I still have the two MJ-808E lights.

In 2021, I bought a powerful MJ-906S 4500-lumen light for my Nordest Albarda. The light was attached to a Garmin handlebar mount, and the battery pack went into a Silca Speed Capsule top tube bag. There are no top-tube mounts on my two Marin Gestalt X12 bikes. A recent Magicshine newsletter announced a new front light - the Hori 1300. It's a unit with a built-in battery and a detachable Garmin base.  It can be attached to the top or bottom of my Syntace Navi Gripper. Front lights, in my opinion, shouldn't haphazardly be clamped to one's handlebars. We ride fancy and often expensive bikes. If you ride a modern bike with fully internal cabling, which I don't and won't, the light you install should be as aero as the bike. My new setup - hella slick!

I retired the carbon plate and MJ-808E setup for my Snakedriver. Instead, I'll be using the powerful MJ-906S light on a Garmin mount with the battery pack going into the Jones H-Bar Pack. I can also carry the Hori 1300 along as a backup. Well, folks, I'm ready for the dark days ahead.

To conclude my post on a different note, let me share how my social media abstinence is going. This summer, I ditched all my social media accounts and then took a further step to reduce my screentime on my phone by deleting all news apps. Thanks to this "appstinence", I now look at the screen of my phone for an average of 15-25 minutes daily. The apps I use most on my 5-year-old iPhone 12 Mini are Maps, Messages, Parcel, pipoDialog for time-tracking, and Strava. AI apps such as ChatGPT have not made it onto my phone. Why should they?
