Videos Shot With The DJI Neo

Patrick April 9, 2025

You can't let it rip when riding downhill with a DJI Neo in tow, particularly in the woods. I slow my speed and adjust my lines with the drone in mind. If branches block the Neo's flight path, I slow way down. If I ride around a tree, I slow down and ride around it the farthest possible. It's not a tool to capture the action, but a great tool to capture the scenery.

The Ittenberg Trail is a nice trail through the woods a few miles from home. It's slightly uphill, so speed isn't high, and the Neo does a marvelous job flying around trees without bumping into them.

On the trail heading east across the Bözingenberg, the DJI Neo has more space to fly, and it's therefore possible to let go of the brakes. As long as I hear the buzz of the little gray hornet, I know it's chasing me.

When riding downhill, you have to ride the trail as if you were a bike guide guiding a beginner down the hill. After all, you can't risk the Neo colliding with a branch on a trail like the Weisse Mauer. If it crashes towards the downhill side of the mountain, you may never find it again.
